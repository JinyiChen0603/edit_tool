## 题目
\textbf{Problem Background.}  
In an acoustically treated room shaped as a regular hexagon $ABCDEF$ with wall length $a$, sound waves propagate along straight-line paths and undergo specular reflection upon striking the walls (that is, the angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection).

\medskip

\textbf{Given Conditions:}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Initial Position.}  
  A sound emitter is located at the midpoint $M$ of wall $AB$.

  \item \textbf{Motion Path.}  
  The sound wave is emitted at an initial angle $\theta$ relative to wall $AB$, where $0<\theta<\pi$.
  The wave reflects successively from the walls
  \[
    BC,\; CD,\; DE,\; EF,\; \text{and } FA
  \]
  in this order, and then returns to wall $AB$.

  \item \textbf{Return Constraint.}  
  The point at which the wave returns to wall $AB$ must lie strictly between $A$ and $M$
  (that is, within the open segment $AM$).

  \item \textbf{Collision Constraint.}  
  All reflections must occur strictly at interior points of the walls, excluding vertices.
\end{enumerate}

\medskip

\textbf{Problem to Solve.}  
Determine the range of values of the projection angle $\theta$ for which all the above conditions are satisfied.

## 解答
\textbf{Solution.}

As in standard billiard and ray–reflection problems, we apply the unfolding method.
Each specular reflection is replaced by reflecting the room across the wall struck,
so that the actual zigzag path becomes a single straight line.

\medskip

\textbf{Step 1: Coordinate setup.}

Let
\[
A=(0,0), \quad B=(a,0),
\]
with the regular hexagon lying above $AB$.
Then the midpoint is
\[
M=\left(\frac{a}{2},0\right).
\]

\medskip

\textbf{Step 2: Unfolding the reflections.}

Reflect the hexagon successively across
\[
BC,\; CD,\; DE,\; EF,\; FA.
\]
After these five reflections, the original wall $AB$ maps to a horizontal segment $A'B'$
located at height
\[
y=\frac{3\sqrt{3}\,a}{2},
\]
with endpoints
\[
A'=\left(\frac{9a}{2},\,\frac{3\sqrt{3}a}{2}\right), \qquad
B'=\left(\frac{11a}{2},\,\frac{3\sqrt{3}a}{2}\right).
\]

In the unfolded plane, the sound ray travels in a straight line from $M$ at angle $\theta$
and intersects the line containing $A'B'$.

\medskip

\textbf{Step 3: Translating the return constraint.}

Returning to the open segment $AM$ in the original hexagon corresponds, in the unfolded picture,
to the straight ray intersecting the segment
\[
A'M' \subset A'B',
\]
where
\[
M'=\left(5a,\,\frac{3\sqrt{3}a}{2}\right)
\]
is the image of $M$ after unfolding.

Thus, the admissible directions are those strictly between the rays $MA'$ and $MM'$.

\medskip

\textbf{Step 4: Boundary angles.}

Compute the slopes of these limiting rays:

\[
\tan\theta_{A'}=
\frac{\frac{3\sqrt{3}a}{2}}{\frac{9a}{2}-\frac{a}{2}}
=\frac{3\sqrt{3}}{8},
\]

\[
\tan\theta_{M'}=
\frac{\frac{3\sqrt{3}a}{2}}{5a-\frac{a}{2}}
=\frac{3\sqrt{3}}{9}
=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}.
\]

\medskip

\textbf{Final Answer.}

Therefore, the range of admissible projection angles is
\[
\boxed{
\arctan\!\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}\right)
\;<\;
\theta
\;<\;
\arctan\!\left(\frac{3\sqrt{3}}{8}\right)
}
\]

## 答案
\[\arctan\!\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}\right)
\;<\;
\theta
\;<\;
\arctan\!\left(\frac{3\sqrt{3}}{8}\right)\]
