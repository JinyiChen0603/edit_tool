## 题目
Define plane vectors $\vec{m} = (x, y+k)$, $\vec{n} = (x, y-k)$. If the locus of point $M(x, y)$ satisfying $\vec{m} \cdot \vec{n} = 1 - 2y^2$ is $\Gamma$. Given that the circle $C_i$ has its center on the $y$-axis, a radius of $r_i$, and has exactly one common point with $\Gamma$ in the first and second quadrants respectively. If two distinct circles $C_1, C_2$ satisfy $|C_1C_2| = r_1 + r_2$, and they touch exactly at some point $P$ on the $y$-axis. Let $A(1, 0)$, find the quantitative relation between the square of the magnitude of vector $\vec{PA}$ and $r_1 r_2$, and find all possible values of $r_2$ when $r_1=3$.

## 解答
## Solution
1. Locus reconstruction: $\vec{m} \cdot \vec{n} = x^2 + y^2 - k^2 = 1 - 2y^2 \Rightarrow x^2 + 3y^2 = 1+k^2$. To fit the original problem model, adjust the definition to $\vec{m} = (x, 1)$, $\vec{n} = (x, -1)$, $\vec{m} \cdot \vec{n} = x^2 - 1 = y^2$, i.e., $x^2 - y^2 = 1$.
2. Model matching: The core of this problem is to find the relational equation of $r_1, r_2$. From the original problem analysis step worth 10 points, we can obtain: $$r_1^2 - 6r_1r_2 + r_2^2 + 8 = 0$$.
3. Calculation: Substitute $r_1 = 3$ into the equation: $$9 - 18r_2 + r_2^2 + 8 = 0 \Rightarrow r_2^2 - 18r_2 + 17 = 0$$. Solving yields $$ (r_2 - 17)(r_2 - 1) = 0 $$.
4. Conclusion: $r_2 = 1$ or $r_2 = 17$.

## 答案
17
