## 题目
Consider a rectangle ( ABCD ) whose side lengths measure $11$ and $12$. An Isosceles triangle is positioned such that none of its points lie outside the rectangle ( ABCD ). Determine the maximum area of such a triangle, which can be expressed in the form ($p\sqrt{q} - r$), where ($p$), ($q$), and ($r$) are positive integers, and ($q$) is not divisible by the square of any prime number. Calculate the value of ($p + q + r$).

## 解答
Solution Process
1. Geometric Transformation and Angle Deduction
Since $ \angle BAD = 90^\circ $ and $ \angle EAF = 60^\circ $, we have:
$$ \angle DAF + \angle BAE = 90^\circ - 60^\circ = 30^\circ $$

Rotate $\triangle ADF$ clockwise by $60^\circ$ around point A.
- Point F lands at point E;
- Point D lands at point $D'$;
- $AD = AD'$, and $ \angle DAD' = 60^\circ$, so $\triangle ADD'$ is an equilateral triangle;
- $ \angle DAF = \angle D'AE$.
From this, we obtain:
$$ \angle BAD' = \angle BAE + \angle D'AE = \angle BAE + \angle DAF = 30^\circ $$

2. Proving Four Points are Concyclic
Consider quadrilateral ABED':
- It is given that $\angle ABE = 90^\circ$;
- By the properties of rotation, $\angle ADF = 90^\circ$ rotated by $60^\circ$ yields $\angle AD'E = 90^\circ$.
Because the angles $\angle ABE$ and $\angle AD'E$ subtending the same side are both $90^\circ$, the points $A, B, E, D'$ are concyclic.
The diameter of this circle is $AE = s$. Let the center be $O$, then the radius $r_{circle} = \frac{s}{2}$.

3. Segment Length Relationship
In circle $O$, according to the Inscribed Angle Theorem, the relationship between the central angle $\angle BOD'$ and the inscribed angle $\angle BAD'$ is:
$$ \angle BOD' = 2 \angle BAD' = 2 \times 30^\circ = 60^\circ $$
Connect $OB, OD'$. Since $OB = OD' = r_{circle}$, $\triangle OBD'$ is an equilateral triangle.
Therefore:
$$ BD' = OB = \frac{s}{2} $$

4. Calculating $BD'$ Using the Law of Cosines
In $\triangle ABD'$:
- $AB = 11$
- $AD' = AD = 12$
- $\angle BAD' = 30^\circ$
By the Law of Cosines:
$$ BD'^2 = AB^2 + AD'^2 - 2 \cdot AB \cdot AD' \cdot \cos 30^\circ $$
$$ BD'^2 = 11^2 + 12^2 - 2 \cdot 11 \cdot 12 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} $$
$$ BD'^2 = 121 + 144 - 132\sqrt{3} $$

$$ BD'^2 = 265 - 132\sqrt{3} $$

5. Algebraic Transformation and Evaluation
From $BD' = \frac{s}{2}$, we obtain:
$$ \left( \frac{s}{2} \right)^2 = 265 - 132\sqrt{3} $$
$$ \frac{s^2}{4} = 265 - 132\sqrt{3} $$
Multiply both sides of the equation by $\sqrt{3}$ to match the form required by the problem:
$$ \frac{s^2\sqrt{3}}{4} = 265\sqrt{3} - 132(\sqrt{3})^2 $$
$$ \frac{s^2\sqrt{3}}{4} = 265\sqrt{3} - 132 \cdot 3 $$
$$ \frac{s^2\sqrt{3}}{4} = 265\sqrt{3} - 396 $$
Comparing this with the form $\frac{s^2\sqrt{3}}{4} = p\sqrt{3} - q$, we get:
- $p = 265$
- $q = 396$
- $r = 3$ (the fixed coefficient resulting from the expansion of $(\sqrt{3})^2$)

6. Final Result
Calculate $p + q + r$:
$$ p + q + r = 265 + 396 + 3 = 664 $$

---
Answer:
$$ \boxed{664} $$

## 答案
664
