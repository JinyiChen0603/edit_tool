## 题目
Problem: The Dominant Isosceles Trapezoid

Definitions:
Let $P$ be a regular $42$-gon with vertices marked $V_0, V_1, \dots, V_{41}$ in counterclockwise order.
Let $O$ be the geometric center of $P$.
We select a set of $4$ distinct vertices to form a convex quadrilateral $Q$.

Constraints:
The quadrilateral $Q$ must satisfy all of the following conditions:
1. $Q$ is an Isosceles Trapezoid.
2. $Q$ is not a Rectangle.
3. $Q$ Strictly Encloses the Center: The center $O$ lies strictly inside $Q$.
4. $Q$ is Strictly Internal: No side of $Q$ (neither base nor leg) coincides with a side of the regular $42$-gon. (Equivalently, the arc length between any adjacent vertices of $Q$ is $\ge 2$).
5. The Dominance Condition: The arc length of the legs of the trapezoid must be strictly greater than the arc length of the longer base. $\text{Arc}(\text{Leg}) > \max(\text{Arc}(\text{Base}_1), \text{Arc}(\text{Base}_2))$.

Question:
Calculate the total number of such quadrilaterals $Q$.

## 解答
Analysis

Let $n = 42$. The semi-perimeter is $n/2 = 21$.
We classify isosceles trapezoids by their axis of symmetry. There are $n/2 = 21$ axes of Type 1 (Vertex-Vertex) and $21$ axes of Type 2 (Edge-Edge).

To strictly enclose the center, the two bases must lie on opposite sides of the diameter perpendicular to the axis. Let $x$ and $y$ be the offsets (indices distances) of the Upper Base and Lower Base from the poles.

Type 1 Axes (Passing through opposite vertices)
Consider the axis through $V_0$ and $V_{21}$.
The bases are chords perpendicular to this axis.
- Upper Base ($B_u$): Defined by offset $x$ from $V_0$. Vertices are $V_x$, $V_{42-x}$. Arc length $L_u = 2x$.
- Lower Base ($B_l$): Defined by offset $y$ from $V_{21}$. Vertices are $V_{21-y}$, $V_{21+y}$. Arc length $L_l = 2y$.
- Leg ($L_{leg}$): Connects $V_x$ to $V_{21-y}$. The arc length is the distance from $V_0$ to $V_{21}$ minus the offsets:
$$ \text{Arc}(\text{Leg}) = 21 - (x + y) $$

Applying Constraints:
1. Strictly Internal: Bases and Legs must be > 1. $2x \ge 2 \Rightarrow x \ge 1$. $2y \ge 2 \Rightarrow y \ge 1$. $21 - (x+y) \ge 2 \Rightarrow x+y \le 19$.
2. Dominance Condition:
$\text{Arc}(\text{Leg}) > \text{Arc}(\text{Upper})$ AND $\text{Arc}(\text{Leg}) > \text{Arc}(\text{Lower})$.
$$ 21 - (x+y) > 2x \Rightarrow 3x + y < 21 $$
$$ 21 - (x+y) > 2y \Rightarrow x + 3y < 21 $$

Counting Lattice Points:
We need pairs $(x,y)$ satisfying:
$x \ge 1, y \ge 1$ and $3x+y \le 20$ and $x+3y \le 20$.
Since the constraints are symmetric, we iterate $x$:
- $x=1$: $3(1)+y \le 20 \Rightarrow y \le 17$. $1+3y \le 20 \Rightarrow 3y \le 19 \Rightarrow y \le 6$. Intersection: $1 \le y \le 6$. (6 points).
- $x=2$: $6+y \le 20 \Rightarrow y \le 14$. $2+3y \le 20 \Rightarrow 3y \le 18 \Rightarrow y \le 6$. Intersection: $1 \le y \le 6$. (6 points).
- $x=3$: $9+y \le 20 \Rightarrow y \le 11$. $3+3y \le 20 \Rightarrow 3y \le 17 \Rightarrow y \le 5$. Intersection: $1 \le y \le 5$. (5 points).
- $x=4$: $12+y \le 20$. $4+3y \le 20 \Rightarrow 3y \le 16 \Rightarrow y \le 5$. Intersection: $1 \le y \le 5$. (5 points).
- $x=5$: $15+y \le 20$. $5+3y \le 20 \Rightarrow 3y \le 15 \Rightarrow y \le 5$. Intersection: $1 \le y \le 5$. (5 points).
- $x=6$: $18+y \le 20 \Rightarrow y \le 2$. $6+3y \le 20 \Rightarrow y \le 4$. Intersection: $1 \le y \le 2$. (2 points).
- $x=7$: $21+y \le 20$. Impossible.

Total Candidates: 29.

Subtract Rectangles:
Rectangles occur at $x=y$. We check which satisfy the inequalities.
Condition: $3x+x \le 20 \Rightarrow 4x \le 20 \Rightarrow x \le 5$.
Valid Rectangles: $x \in \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5\}$. (5 points).

Valid Type 1: $29 - 5 = 24$.
Total for 21 axes: $21 \times 24 = 504$.

Type 2 Axes (Passing through midpoints of edges)
Axis through edge midpoints. The offsets $x$, $y$ represent "rungs" from the center.
- Base Arcs: $L_u = 2x-1$ and $L_l = 2y-1$.
- Leg Arc: $21 - (x+y)$.

Applying Constraints:
1. Strictly Internal:
$2x-1 \ge 2 \Rightarrow 2x \ge 3 \Rightarrow x \ge 2$.
$2y-1 \ge 2 \Rightarrow y \ge 2$.
2. Dominance Condition:
$21 - (x+y) > 2x-1 \Rightarrow 3x + y < 22 \Rightarrow 3x+y \le 21$.
$21 - (x+y) > 2y-1 \Rightarrow x + 3y < 22 \Rightarrow x+3y \le 21$.

Counting Lattice Points ($x,y \ge 2$):
- $x=2$: $6+y \le 21 \Rightarrow y \le 15$. $2+3y \le 21 \Rightarrow 3y \le 19 \Rightarrow y \le 6$. Range $2 \le y \le 6$. (5 points).
- $x=3$: $9+y \le 21$. $3+3y \le 21 \Rightarrow 3y \le 18 \Rightarrow y \le 6$. Range $2 \le y \le 6$. (5 points).
- $x=4$: $12+y \le 21$. $4+3y \le 21 \Rightarrow 3y \le 17 \Rightarrow y \le 5$. Range $2 \le y \le 5$. (4 points).
- $x=5$: $15+y \le 21$. $5+3y \le 21 \Rightarrow 3y \le 16 \Rightarrow y \le 5$. Range $2 \le y \le 5$. (4 points).
- $x=6$: $18+y \le 21 \Rightarrow y \le 3$. $6+3y \le 21 \Rightarrow y \le 5$. Range $2 \le y \le 3$. (2 points).
- $x=7$: $21+y \le 21 \Rightarrow y \le 0$. Impossible.

Total Candidates: 20.

Subtract Rectangles ($x=y$):
Condition: $3x+x \le 21 \Rightarrow 4x \le 21 \Rightarrow x \le 5.25$.
Valid $x \in \{2, 3, 4, 5\}$. (4 points).

Valid Type 2: $20 - 4 = 16$.
Total for 21 axes: $21 \times 16 = 336$.

Final Summation
$$
\text{Total} = 504 \;(\text{Type 1}) + 336 \;(\text{Type 2}) = 840
$$

## 答案
$$840$$
