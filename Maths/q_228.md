## 题目
Problem: The Damped Lattice Flux (Single Objective)

Definitions:
Let $S_6$ be the set of integer points in a $6 \times 6$ grid:
$$
S_6 = \{(x,y) \in \mathbb{Z}^2 \mid 1 \le x, y \le 6\}
$$
We select an ordered pair of distinct points $(A, B)$ from $S_6$:
$$
A = (x_1, y_1), \quad B = (x_2, y_2)
$$

Question:
Let the function $F(A, B)$ be defined as:
$$
F(A, B) = (x_1 y_2 - x_2 y_1) - 2\left(|x_1 - x_2| + |y_1 - y_2|\right)
$$
Find the number of distinct ordered pairs $(A, B)$ that achieve the maximum possible value of $F(A, B)$ on the set $S_6$.

## 解答
To find the number of pairs maximizing $F(A, B)$, we must first determine the global maximum value of the function.

1. Maximization Strategy
The function is $F = \det(A, B) - 2 d_1(A, B)$.
To maximize $F$, we need a large determinant and a small Manhattan distance. However, the coefficient $-2$ heavily penalizes distance.
Assuming the optimal configuration satisfies $x_1 \ge x_2$ and $y_2 \ge y_1$, we simplify:
$$ F = x_1 y_2 - x_2 y_1 - 2(x_1 - x_2) - 2(y_2 - y_1) $$

$$ F = x_1(y_2 - 2) - y_1(x_2 - 2) + 2x_2 - 2y_2 $$

2. Finding the Global Maximum
We test the boundaries where $x_1$ and $y_2$ are maximized ($x_1=6, y_2=6$).
$$ F = 6(4) - y_1(x_2 - 2) + 2x_2 - 12 = 12 + 2x_2 - y_1(x_2 - 2) $$

Let $g(x_2, y_1) = 2x_2 - y_1(x_2 - 2)$.
We analyze the critical cases for $x_2$:
- Case $x_2 > 2$: The term $(x_2-2)$ is positive. To maximize $g$, we minimize $y_1$ (set $y_1=1$).
$$ g(x_2, 1) = 2x_2 - (x_2 - 2) = x_2 + 2 $$
Maximized when $x_2=6$.
Value: $6+2=8$. Total $F = 12+8 = 20$.
Configuration: $A=(6,1)$ and $B=(6,6)$.

- Case $x_2 = 1$: The term $(x_2-2)$ is $-1$.
$$ g(1, y_1) = 2 + y_1 $$
Maximized when $y_1=6$.
Value: $2+6=8$. Total $F = 12+8 = 20$.
Configuration: $A=(6,6)$ and $B=(1,6)$.

- Case $x_2 = 2$: The interaction term vanishes.
$$ g(2, y_1) = 4 $$
Total $F = 12+4=16$. Sub-optimal.

3. Counting the Solutions
The maximum value is verified to be 20. The configurations achieving this are:
- $A=(6,1)$ and $B=(6,6)$
- $A=(6,6)$ and $B=(1,6)$
Both pairs are distinct and within $S_6$. No other boundary or internal configurations reach 20 due to the steep distance penalty.

Conclusion:
There are exactly 2 such pairs.

## 答案
$$2$$
