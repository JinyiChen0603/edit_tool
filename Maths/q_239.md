## 题目
Determine all positive integers $n$ such that $2^n - 1$ is a factor of $m^2 + 289$ for some integer $m$.

## 解答
On the one hand, if $n$ has an odd factor $s > 1$, then $2^s - 1$ is a factor of $2^n - 1 = (2^s)^{\frac{n}{s}} - 1 = (2^s - 1)\left[(2^s)^{\frac{n}{s}-1} + (2^s)^{\frac{n}{s}-2} + \dots + (2^s) + 1\right]$. If $2^n - 1$ is a factor of $m^2 + 289$, then $2^s - 1$ is a factor of $m^2 + 289$. Because $3 \nmid (2^s - 1)$, $17 \nmid (2^s - 1)$, and $2^s - 1 \equiv -1 \pmod{4}$, Therefore, $2^s - 1$ must have a prime factor $p > 3$, and $p \equiv -1 \pmod{4}$. Thus, $m^2 \equiv -289 \pmod{p}$. Raising both sides to the power of $\frac{p-1}{2}$ gives $m^{p-1} \equiv -17^{p-1} \pmod{p}$. By Fermat's Little Theorem, we have $1 \equiv -1 \pmod{p}$, i.e., $0 \equiv 2 \pmod{p}$, a contradiction. 

On the other hand, $2^n-1 = (2^{2^{k-1}}+1)(2^{2^{k-1}}-1) = \dots = (2^{2^{k-1}}+1)(2^{2^{k-2}}+1)\dots(2^{2^j}+1)(2^{2^j}-1)\dots(2^2+1)(2^2-1)(2+1)(2-1)$.

The congruence equation $x^2 \equiv -1 \pmod{(2^{2^j} + 1)}$ has a solution $x \equiv 2^{2^j} \pmod{(2^{2^j} + 1)}$. And when $j > i$, $(2^{2^i} + 1,2^{2^j} + 1) = (2^{2^m} - 1,2^{2^j} + 1) = \dots = (2^{2^i} - 1,2^{2^i} + 1) = 1$. Therefore, according to the Chinese Remainder Theorem, the system of congruences $x \equiv 2^{2^j} \pmod{(2^{2^j} + 1)}$ ($j = 1,2,\dots,k - 1$) has a solution $x_0$. Let $m = 17x_0$. Then $m^2 + 289 = 289(x_0^2 + 1)$ is divisible by $2^n - 1$.

## 答案
$2^k$
