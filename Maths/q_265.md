## 题目
In pharmacokinetics, the concentration $f(x)$ of a drug in the bloodstream (in mg/L) at time $x \ge \frac{1}{2}$ hours after administration is modeled by $f(x) = (x - \sqrt{2x - 1})e^{\alpha x}$, where $\alpha$ is a metabolic rate constant. Determine the possible range of the drug concentration $f(x)$ over the time interval $\left[\frac{1}{2}, +\infty\right)$.

## 解答
From
\[
f'(x) = \frac{(1-x)(\sqrt{2x-1}-2)e^{-x}}{\sqrt{2x-1}} = 0,
\]
Solving gives
\[
x = 1 \text{ or } x = \frac{5}{2}.
\]
Because

| $x$ | $\frac{1}{2}$ | $\left(\frac{1}{2},1\right)$ | $1$ | $\left(1,\frac{5}{2}\right)$ | $\frac{5}{2}$ | $\left(\frac{5}{2},+\infty\right)$ |
|-----|---------------|------------------------------|-----|----------------------------|---------------|-----------------------------------|
| $f'(x)$ |  | $-$ | $0$ | $+$ | $0$ | $-$ |
| $f(x)$ | $\frac{1}{2}e^{-\frac{1}{2}}$ | $\downarrow$ | $0$ | $\uparrow$ | $\frac{1}{2}e^{-\frac{5}{2}}$ | $\downarrow$ |

And $f(x) = \frac{1}{2}(\sqrt{2x - 1} - 1)^2e^{-x} \ge 0$,

So the range of $f(x)$ on the interval $\left[\frac{1}{2},+\infty\right)$ is $\left[0, \frac{1}{2}e^{-\frac{1}{2}}\right]$.

Note: This solution does not fully consider the parameter $\alpha$ classification. For complete analysis, we need to discuss cases based on $\alpha$:
- When $0 \le \alpha \le 8$: function decreases then increases, range is $[0,+\infty)$.
- When $\alpha \le 0$: function decreases, increases, then decreases.
- When $\alpha \ge 8$: function decreases, increases, decreases, then increases.

## 答案
$[0, \frac12 e^{-\frac12}]$
