## 题目
In the Cartesian coordinate system $xOy$, let $AB$ be a chord of the parabola $y^2 = 4x$ passing through the point $F(1, 0)$. The circumcircle of $\triangle AOB$ intersects the parabola at a point $P$ (distinct from points $O$, $A$, and $B$). If $PF$ bisects $\angle APB$, find all possible values of $|PF|$.

## 解答
Solution
Let $A\left(\frac{y_1^2}{4}, y_1\right)$, $B\left(\frac{y_2^2}{4}, y_2\right)$, and $P\left(\frac{y_3^2}{4}, y_3\right)$. From the conditions, we know that $y_1$, $y_2$, and $y_3$ are pairwise distinct and non-zero.
Let the equation of the line $AB$ be $x = ty + 1$. Combining this with the equation of the parabola, we obtain
$$ y^2 - 4ty - 4 = 0, $$
Thus
$$ y_1 y_2 = -4. $$
Note that the circumcircle of $\triangle AOB$ passes through point $O$. Let the equation of this circle be
$$ x^2 + y^2 + dx + ey = 0, $$
Combining this with $x = \frac{y^2}{4}$, we get $\frac{y^4}{16} + \left(1 + \frac{d}{4}\right)y^2 + ey = 0$. This quartic equation has four distinct real roots: $y = y_1$, $y_2$, $y_3$, and $0$. Thus, by Vieta's theorem, $y_1 + y_2 + y_3 + 0 = 0$. Consequently,
$$ y_3 = -(y_1 + y_2). $$
Since $PF$ bisects $\angle APB$, according to the Angle Bisector Theorem, we have
$$ \frac{|PA|}{|PB|} = \frac{|FA|}{|FB|} = \frac{|y_1|}{|y_2|}, $$
Combining (1) and (2), we have
$$ \frac{y_1^2}{y_2^2} = \frac{|PA|^2}{|PB|^2} = \frac{\left(\frac{y_3^2}{4} - \frac{y_1^2}{4}\right)^2 + (y_3 - y_1)^2}{\left(\frac{y_3^2}{4} - \frac{y_2^2}{4}\right)^2 + (y_3 - y_2)^2} $$
$$ = \frac{((y_1 + y_2)^2 - y_1^2)^2 + 16(2y_1 + y_2)^2}{((y_1 + y_2)^2 - y_2^2)^2 + 16(2y_2 + y_1)^2} $$
$$ = \frac{(y_2^2 - 8)^2 + 16(4y_1^2 + y_2^2 - 16)}{(y_1^2 - 8)^2 + 16(4y_2^2 + y_1^2 - 16)} = \frac{y_2^4 + 64y_1^2 - 192}{y_1^4 + 64y_2^2 - 192}, $$
That is, $y_1^6 + 64y_1^2y_2^2 - 192y_1^2 = y_2^6 + 64y_2^2y_1^2 - 192y_2^2$. Thus,
$$(y_1^2 - y_2^2)(y_1^4 + y_1^2y_2^2 + y_2^4 - 192) = 0.$$
When $y_1^2 = y_2^2$, we have $y_2 = -y_1$, so $y_3 = 0$. In this case, $P$ coincides with $O$, which contradicts the condition.
When $y_1^4 + y_1^2y_2^2 + y_2^4 - 192 = 0$, noting (1), we have
$$ (y_1^2 + y_2^2)^2 = 192 + (y_1y_2)^2 = 208. $$
Since $y_1^2 + y_2^2 = 4\sqrt{13} > 8 = |2y_1y_2|$, there exist real numbers $y_1$ and $y_2$ satisfying (1) and $y_1^2 + y_2^2 = 4\sqrt{13}$, which correspond to points $A$ and $B$ that meet the conditions. In this case, combining (1) and (2), we know
$$ |PF| = \frac{y_3^2}{4} + 1 = \frac{(y_1 + y_2)^2 + 4}{4} = \frac{y_1^2 + y_2^2 - 4}{4} $$
$$ = \frac{\sqrt{208} - 4}{4} = \sqrt{13} - 1. $$

## 答案
$$\sqrt{13} - 1$$
