## 题目
Problem: The Lattice Deviation Limit (Single Objective)

Definitions:
Let $S_5$ be the set of integer points in a $5 \times 5$ grid:
$$
S_5 = \{(x,y) \in \mathbb{Z}^2 \mid 1 \le x, y \le 5\}
$$
We select an ordered pair of distinct points $(A, B)$ from $S_5$:
$$
A = (x_1, y_1), \quad B = (x_2, y_2)
$$

Question:
Let the function $D(A, B)$ be defined as the determinant minus the Manhattan distance:
$$
D(A, B) = (x_1 y_2 - x_2 y_1) - (|x_1 - x_2| + |y_1 - y_2|)
$$
Find the number of distinct ordered pairs $(A, B)$ that achieve the maximum possible integer value of $D(A, B)$ on the set $S_5$.

## 解答
Analysis
We aim to maximize the function $D = (x_1 y_2 - x_2 y_1) - (|x_1 - x_2| + |y_1 - y_2|)$ on the grid $1 \le x, y \le 5$.

1. Heuristic for Maximization
To maximize $D$, we need the determinant term $(x_1 y_2 - x_2 y_1)$ to be as large as possible, while keeping the distance term $(|x_1 - x_2| + |y_1 - y_2|)$ relatively small.
The determinant is maximized when $A$ and $B$ are close to the coordinate axes (e.g., $A$ near the X-axis, $B$ near the Y-axis) and have large magnitudes.
We assume the configuration $x_1 > x_2$ and $y_2 > y_1$, which makes the determinant positive and simplifies the absolute values:
$$ D = x_1 y_2 - x_2 y_1 - (x_1 - x_2) - (y_2 - y_1) $$

2. Boundary Optimization
Let's fix the outer boundaries to maximize the positive product $x_1 y_2$. The maximum coordinate in $S_5$ is 5.
Set $x_1 = 5$ and $y_2 = 5$.
Substitute these into the simplified equation:
$$
D = 5(5) - x_2 y_1 - (5 - x_2) - (5 - y_1) \\
D = 25 - x_2 y_1 - 5 + x_2 - 5 + y_1 \\
D = 15 + x_2 + y_1 - x_2 y_1
$$
We can factor the variable part by adding and subtracting 1:
$$
D = 16 - 1 + x_2 + y_1 - x_2 y_1 \\
D = 16 - (1 - x_2 - y_1 + x_2 y_1) \\
D = 16 - (x_2 - 1)(y_1 - 1)
$$

3. Finding the Maximum
The term $(x_2 - 1)(y_1 - 1)$ is non-negative since $x_2, y_1 \ge 1$.
To maximize $D$, we must minimize this subtrahend. The minimum value is 0.
Thus, the theoretical maximum is:
$$ D_{\max} = 16 - 0 = 16 $$
This maximum is achieved if and only if $(x_2 - 1)(y_1 - 1) = 0$.

4. Enumerating the Solutions
The condition $(x_2 - 1)(y_1 - 1) = 0$ implies either $x_2 = 1$ or $y_1 = 1$.
We must also respect the grid constraints ($1 \le x_2, y_1 \le 5$) and the distinctness of points $A$ and $B$.
With $x_1 = 5$, $y_2 = 5$, the points are $A($5$, $y_1$) and $B($x_2$, 5)$.

Case A: $x_2 = 1$
Points are $A($5$, $y_1$) and $B($1$, 5$).
$y_1$ can be any integer from 1 to 5.
- If $y_1 = 5$, $A=$($5$,$5$), $B=$($1$,$5$). Distinct? Yes. Valid.
- Pairs: ($5$,$1$)-($1$,$5$), ($5$,$2$)-($1$,$5$), ($5$,$3$)-($1$,$5$), ($5$,$4$)-($1$,$5$), ($5$,$5$)-($1$,$5$).
- Count: $5$ pairs.

Case B: $y_1 = 1$
Points are $A($5$, 1$) and $B($x_2$, 5$).
$x_2$ can be any integer from 1 to 5.
- If $x_2 = 5$, $A=$($5$,1), $B=$($5$,$5$). Distinct? Yes. Valid.
- Pairs: ($5$,$1$)-($1$,$5$), ($5$,$1$)-($2$,$5$), ($5$,$1$)-($3$,$5$), ($5$,$1$)-($4$,$5$), ($5$,$1$)-($5$,$5$).
- Count: $5$ pairs.

Overlap:
The pair where both $x_2 = 1$ and $y_1 = 1$ is counted in both cases.
This is the pair $A($5$, 1$) and $B($1$, 5$)$.
We must subtract this duplicate.

Total Count:
$5 + 5 - 1 = 9$

Conclusion:
There are $9$ distinct ordered pairs that achieve the maximum deviation of $16$.

## 答案
$$9$$
